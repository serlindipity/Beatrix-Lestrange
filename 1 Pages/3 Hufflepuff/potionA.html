Chapter Three | The Results

’ve decided to follow my instincts and add only one mandrake root and heat it for 10 seconds to preserve the herbs and avoid over-cooking it. I proceed with my potion and hope for the best.

“Beatrix Lestrange, come forth.” I was one of the last students to finish up and called up to be evaluated. The professor called me in front with my pepper-up potion.

I nodded and walked down to his table. He scoops a spoon from my potion and he confusedly looked at me

“How come your potion taste different from the others? I can distinctly taste the bicorn horn and the mandrake? They’re not overcooked and stand on their own.”

“Yes, professor. I saw that some of the instructions are wrong and may sabotage the herbs and ingredients of the potion. So, I made this potion and followed my instincts.” I confidently replied

“Classed is now dismissed. No one except Lestrange had the accurate copy of the potion I asked you to make. Now leave.” He silently contemplated and announced.

I was cleaning up my work space when he approached me

“Beatrix Lestrange, you are very talented in potion making. You know by heart how to treat the herbs and the potions whole-heartedly. Marvelous indeed. Here please accept the reward I bestow upon you. The Felix Felicis potion. Congratulations.” He patted my shoulder and left.
